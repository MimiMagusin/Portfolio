Mimi Magusin

Hi! I am Mimi, and I am a developer and teacher at Codaisseur, a coding school in Amsterdam. At work, I am developing the front and back end of our website, the content and technical aspects of our learning materials and I teach our students all sorts of skills and topics in HTML, CSS and JavaScript.

I love the creativity that is involved with creating things, whether it is an app, a musical piece or a lesson. The logic puzzle that is involved with making a result bigger than the sum of its parts, repeating parts of your project effectively, making it easy to do so and writing your code, music or lesson plan in such a way that your presence is not necessary for it to be performed or improved on intrigues me.

I studied Liberal Arts and Sciences at University College Roosevelt with a major in Musical Composition and Musicology (2016, Cum Laude). Besides, I followed as many teaching courses as possible, several courses in the field of anthropology and a course in calculus.

In 2017, I joined the Codaisseur Code Academy, a coding bootcamp introducing me to full stack development. I got inspired and realised that I had many prejudices about code that had prevented me from trying it out earlier, which turned out to be very wrong. I also realised that with great code comes both great power and great potential, and I became passionate about sharing my skills and knowledge with others, especially people with different backgrounds and different ways of thinking - we need diversity for code to reach its full potential!

Currently, I am looking for ways to grow myself, both as a developer and as a teacher. I am working on getting through different topics in Computer Science, to better understand how code gets translated to a computer and how to write code that is easy maintainable and reusable through concepts such as Object Oriented Programming. For teaching, I try to find a balance between improving myself through theory and practice, bringing into practice what I learned at university and evaluating the results of our students, together with the other teachers in our team.
